What the network produces

Challenges that turn human video into robot policy.

Labs order across twelve challenge types, from cheap language grounding to expert model-in-the-loop work. Four of the core tiers are below; the full catalog lives in the order book.

01Tier 1 · Language grounding

Episode-level annotation

A whole trajectory gets one or a few high-level labels: the instruction it satisfies, the objects in scene, and the outcome. Cheapest tier, but it decides how the model maps language to behaviour.

Example lab tasks
  • Write 5–10 distinct instructions that each correctly describe the episode.
  • Tag every object in scene and identify the goal object.
  • Label the outcome success, partial, or failure, with a reason for each failure.
02Tier 2 · Temporal segmentation

Action labelling

Decompose the episode into atomic skills with timestamps: reach, grasp, transport, place, release. The hierarchy unlocks long-horizon tasks and skill recombination.

Example lab tasks
  • Segment the episode using a fixed verb list of motion primitives.
  • Mark the exact start and end frame of each primitive.
  • Label left- and right-hand tracks independently when they overlap.
03Tier 1 · Data hygiene

Clipping

Cut raw continuous recordings into clean units: trim dead time, split sessions into episodes, and strip scene-reset and fumbling segments before any labelling begins.

Example lab tasks
  • Trim each recording to the moment the task starts and finishes.
  • Split a 10-minute session into individual pick-and-place episodes.
  • Remove every scene-reset segment between attempts.
04Tier 3 · Model-in-the-loop

Hand pose estimation

Per-frame 3D hand, finger, and object keypoints turn human video into joint angles a robot gripper can copy. It is the bridge from cheap human demos to robot policy.

Example lab tasks
  • Correct the 21 predicted hand keypoints on frames where the estimator drifted.
  • Label per-frame contact state: which fingers touch the object.
  • Verify 6-DoF object pose so the grasp can be retargeted to a robot.

Two roles keep the network honest

Collection and verification are separated and independently rewarded, so the people producing data are never the ones grading it.

⛏

Miners

Anyone, anywhere

A global, permissionless workforce that collects and annotates. Miners claim hours from open orders, produce annotations with their own tools, and earn per accepted unit.

  • Claim hours from any open order their trust tier allows.
  • Submit structured output in the order's required delivery format.
  • Build a trust score that unlocks higher-paying tiers over time.
✓

Validators

Stake to verify

The quality layer that fact-checks every submission and decides what the network accepts and how emissions are split. Validators stake collateral and are rewarded for catching bad work.

  • Inject hidden honeypots and run multi-miner consensus to surface errors.
  • Score submissions against the client's held-out evaluation set.
  • Run geometric checks against calibration to reject impossible labels.

Designed against gaming

Six independent defenses make it cheaper to do the work correctly than to attempt to game the reward.

01

Hidden honeypots

Pre-verified gold clips are seeded invisibly at 10–15% of volume. Failing them drops a miner's trust score and tier.

02

Multi-miner consensus

The same clip is annotated by several miners, so outliers are flagged and disagreements escalate to review.

03

Geometric checks

Trajectory submissions are validated against the stereo calibration file, so physically impossible labels are rejected automatically.

04

Held-out eval scoring

Work is scored on model-performance delta against a hidden evaluation set, not on label count, so volume cannot buy a passing grade.

05

Compounding trust

Trust scores are non-transferable and accrue on-chain, so a clean record cannot be bought, sold, or faked by a fresh account.

06

Randomised assignment & stake

Tasks and honeypots are assigned unpredictably, and validators stake collateral that is slashed for collusion or negligent approval.
