What gaming looks like

The failure modes are predictable, so the defenses are built directly against them.

Lazy labels

Phoning it in

Rushed or copy-pasted annotations that look plausible but teach the model nothing.

Sybil farms

Fake identities

One actor spinning up many accounts to flood orders and dilute quality.

Collusion

Rubber-stamping

Miners and validators colluding to approve each other's bad work.

Volume spam

Quantity over quality

Submitting huge volumes of low-value labels to farm the reward.

( 03 )

Six defenses, working together

No single check is decisive, but together they make honest work cheaper than gaming the reward.

01

Hidden honeypots

Pre-verified gold clips are seeded invisibly at 10–15% of volume, so failing them drops a miner's trust score and tier.

02

Multi-miner consensus

The same clip is annotated by several miners, so outliers are flagged and disagreements escalate to review.

03

Geometric checks

Trajectory submissions are validated against the calibration file, so physically impossible labels are rejected automatically.

04

Held-out eval scoring

Work is scored on model-performance delta against a hidden set, not on label count, so volume cannot buy a passing grade.

05

Compounding trust

Trust scores are non-transferable and accrue on-chain, so a clean record cannot be bought, sold, or faked by a fresh account.

06

Random assignment & stake

Tasks and honeypots are assigned unpredictably, and validators stake collateral that is slashed for collusion or negligence.

( 04 )

Trust is earned, then it compounds

A miner's history is the network's strongest signal, because it is the one thing a new account cannot fake.

  1. 1
    Start at Tier 1
    New miners take entry tasks that are cheap to verify and honeypot-checked.
  2. 2
    Pass to climb
    Every accepted submission lifts trust; failed honeypots pull it back down.
  3. 3
    Unlock higher pay
    Higher trust opens Tier 2 and Tier 3 work at higher rates and a higher barrier.

What happens if you cheat

The penalty is automatic and it compounds, so a single bad streak is expensive to recover from.
